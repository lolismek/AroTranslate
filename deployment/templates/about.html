About AroTranslate

This page is momentarily only available in English.

Aromanian is a unique and endangered Romance language* spoken by over 300,000 people in the Balkans. Despite its rich cultural significance, it is often overlooked in the digital world. The goal of AroTranslate is to help make Aromanian more accessible and engaging through state-of-the-art translation technology.

The lack of government funding and support for the Aromanian culture and tongue is concerning. Through this project, we also aim to raise awareness and advocate for increased recognition and support.

Learn more about AroTranslate on Github or Huggingface.

We are dedicated to supporting the Aromanian community and encourage anyone interested in contributing or learning more to get in touch with us at alex.jerpelea@gmail.com.

*The status of Aromanian as either independent language or Romanian dialect is a heated debate with political implications on both sides. The first view is mainly adopted by modern Romance research at the international level, while the latter is traditionally accepted by Romanian linguists and the pre-war generation. At AroTranslate, we do not officially adhere to any view, but we do refer to Aromanian as a language for simplicity and practicability. We encourage the users to read more about this topic and form a view of their own. We recommend and cite this article. At the end of the day, "the Aromanians have been striving merely to preserve their identity, culture and mother-tongue", and we support this cause.
